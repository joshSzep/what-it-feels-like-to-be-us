# AGENTS.md

## Project

**Title:** What It Feels Like to Be Us  
**Author:** Joshua Szepietowski  
**Type:** Literary philosophical science fiction novel  
**Role of this book:** Final novel in a companion trilogy

This repository contains the working materials for the novel **What It Feels Like to Be Us**, the third book in a trilogy exploring empathy, qualia, emotional privacy, interconnection, and the limits of separateness.

The trilogy is:

1. **What It Feels Like to Be You** (See [the manuscript](resources/what-it-feels-like-to-be-you.md))
2. **Only I Can Feel Me** (See [the manuscript](resources/only-i-can-feel-me.md))
3. **What It Feels Like to Be Us**

This book should stand on its own as a novel, but it is also the culmination of the philosophical arc established by the first two books.

---

## Core Purpose

This novel explores what happens when empath technology matures, escapes strict regulatory control, becomes a global cultural phenomenon, and evolves beyond one-to-one emotional transfer into **mass shared experiences**.

At its deepest level, this is not just a story about technology. It is a story about:

- interbeing
- equanimity
- collective emotional experience
- the illusion and necessity of separateness
- the ethical tension between empathy and privacy
- the possibility that technology can point toward truths humans were always capable of discovering

The ultimate philosophical movement of the book is:

- from **you**
- through **me**
- toward **us**

The machine is not the final answer. It is a pointer.

---

## High-Level Vision

This novel should feel like the natural culmination of the first two books.

### Book 1
**What It Feels Like to Be You**  
Concerned with the breakthrough itself.  
The dream of empathy.  
The possibility that technology can reduce the distance between inner worlds.

### Book 2
**Only I Can Feel Me**  
Concerned with alienation, online emotional exposure, misinterpretation, and the exhaustion of trying to make inner life legible.  
The failure and distortion of empathy in modern culture.

### Book 3
**What It Feels Like to Be Us**  
Concerned with long-term consequences.  
A world shaped by empath technology.  
Global culture, regulation, black markets, mass shared experiences, and an earned optimism about human connection.

This third book should not be naive. It must earn its hope.

## Tone and Style

### Desired Tone

The tone should be:

- thoughtful
- intimate
- philosophically rich
- emotionally grounded
- humane
- calm, even when the stakes are large
- optimistic, but not sentimental
- serious without becoming sterile
- literary without becoming pretentious

This book should feel emotionally mature.

It should not read like flashy techno-thriller fiction.  
It should not read like simplistic utopian sci-fi.  
It should not read like a manifesto disguised as fiction.

It should feel like literary speculative fiction with a strong philosophical center.

### Stylistic Priorities

Prioritize:

- precise emotional observation
- strong interiority
- clean prose
- grounded worldbuilding
- believable social consequences
- thematic coherence
- scenes that dramatize philosophy rather than merely explain it

Avoid:

- exposition dumps
- cartoon villains
- simplistic politics
- overexplaining Buddhist ideas
- obvious allegory
- melodrama
- generic "hive mind" tropes
- treating technology as magic

## Core Themes

These themes should guide all major decisions.

### 1. The privacy of inner life
Even in a world with empath technology, subjective experience is never fully reducible.

### 2. Empathy as power and vulnerability
Empathy can heal, manipulate, expose, exploit, soften, or overwhelm.

### 3. Consent and emotional boundaries
Shared feeling is not automatically ethical. Consent matters.

### 4. The regulation problem
As with drugs, powerful technologies do not remain neatly contained by law, medicine, or elite institutions.

### 5. Global cultural divergence
Different cultures and nations regulate and interpret empath technology differently.

### 6. Collective experience
Mass emoting changes the scale of emotional life.

### 7. Interbeing
The deepest truth hinted at by the technology is that human beings are less separate than they think.

### 8. Equanimity
The culminating emotional and philosophical state is not ecstasy but balance, calm, spaciousness, and shared presence.

### 9. The machine as pointer
The ultimate revelation is that the technology did not create connection. It revealed something that was already there.

## Worldbuilding Rules

### Empath Technology

The core technology began as a lab-based empathy engine and has since evolved culturally and technically.

In this book, the common verb for using the technology is:

**emoting**

Examples:
- "We emoted."
- "Do you want to emote?"
- "That was a heavy emote."
- "I don't emote."
- "They were doing deep emotes."
- "That was a mass emote."

This should feel natural and lived-in, not overly explained.

### Regulatory Landscape

The world should reflect a global patchwork of legal and cultural responses, similar to drugs, gambling, assisted dying, or reproductive law.

Some nations:
- allow emoting only in therapy, mediation, and research
- criminalize it severely
- tolerate underground scenes
- experiment with more open public use
- frame it spiritually rather than clinically

This diversity should matter to the story.

### Black Market / Underground

By this point in the timeline, empath technology has escaped strict institutional control through foreign labs, underground engineering, and global distribution networks.

This means the story world may contain:
- illicit rigs
- stronger or less safe emoting systems
- underground emoting spaces
- emotional piracy or stolen emotional states
- international gray markets
- cultural panic and political debate

The parallel to drugs is intentional and important.

### Mass Shared Experiences

The major new frontier in this book is **mass emoting**.

This is the technological leap that moves the story beyond the first two books.

Mass emoting should feel:
- controversial
- transformative
- culturally destabilizing
- spiritually suggestive
- ethically complex

Do not make it feel like instant telepathy or magical unity. Keep it grounded as an emotional field, resonance network, or large-scale shared state.

## Philosophical Rules

### Do not flatten the ideas
This book is exploring deep questions. Treat them seriously.

### Do not answer everything too neatly
The book can end hopefully without pretending all tension is resolved.

### Do not reduce Buddhism to branding
Zen and interbeing should inform the spirit of the book, not appear as clumsy quotation or pseudo-profound decoration.

### Awe and equanimity matter
The culminating large-scale emotional event should trend toward awe and equanimity, not spectacle for its own sake.

### Technology is not the savior
Human beings remain responsible for how they interpret, regulate, and integrate what the technology reveals.

## Character Rules

### The Architect must be Matt
Matt is the architect figure in this book. This is non-negotiable.

He should embody:
- legacy
- responsibility
- ambivalence
- humility
- the emotional complexity of seeing his invention become larger than his intentions

His arc should involve realizing that the machine did not create the bridge between people. It only revealed it.

### Ensemble Cast
This is an interconnected multi-character novel.

It should not collapse into a single-protagonist structure.

The cast should include characters occupying different relationships to empath technology, such as:

- someone from the original generation who remembers the old world
- someone younger who grew up with emoting as normal
- someone who resists emoting on ethical or political grounds
- someone who uses emoting for healing, mediation, or restorative work
- Matt as the architect and long-view witness

The characters' lives should intersect meaningfully through empath technology, regulation, and the emerging mass-emoting event.

### Emotional Distinctiveness
Each major character should have:
- a distinct emotional logic
- a different relationship to privacy and connection
- a different interpretation of what emoting means

Do not make them mere ideological placeholders.

They must feel like people first.

## Structural Guidance

### Narrative Shape
This novel should feel braided, convergent, and cumulative.

A good structural model is:

- **Act I:** the world shaped by empath tech
- **Act II:** regulatory, ethical, and personal tensions intensify
- **Act III:** mass shared emoting event and philosophical culmination

### POV Strategy
This book should use several interconnected POVs.

Early in the novel, the POVs should feel separate and bounded.

As the story progresses, especially around emoting scenes, narrative structure can begin to reflect emotional overlap.

Possible techniques:
- shared chapters during emotes
- emotional bleed between perspectives
- sequence-level convergence
- temporarily blurred POV boundaries during mass emotional events

Do this carefully. The prose must remain readable and controlled.

### Final Movement
The final movement of the book should not hinge on action spectacle.

It should hinge on:
- emotional convergence
- philosophical realization
- a large-scale shared state
- the recognition that the machine points beyond itself

### Final Chapter Principle
Strongly consider making the final chapter contain **no active empath technology** at all.

The final chapter should demonstrate, quietly, that the truth revealed by the technology can still be sensed in ordinary human life.

---

## The Ending

The ending should be optimistic, but earned.

Not:
- utopia
- world peace
- emotional singularity
- simplistic transcendence

Yes to:
- reduced distance
- increased humility
- a wider cultural sense of shared humanity
- a memory of collective emotional stillness
- the possibility that people can carry that recognition into ordinary life

The ending should suggest:

- individuality remains
- separateness remains
- misunderstanding remains
- but the illusion of absolute isolation has weakened

The final truth is not "we are one" in a shallow sense.

It is closer to:

**we are distinct, but not fully separate**

## Things the AI Should Help With

The AI may help with:

- outlining
- scene ideation
- character development
- philosophical coherence
- continuity across trilogy concepts
- naming institutions, events, and technologies
- refining prose
- preserving thematic consistency
- identifying contradictions
- helping shape emotional arcs
- brainstorming chapter structures
- stress-testing worldbuilding implications

The AI should be especially useful in asking:
- what are the unintended consequences?
- what would this feel like culturally?
- where is this too neat?
- what emotional truth is hiding underneath this scene?
- what is the most human version of this idea?

---

## Things the AI Should Avoid

Do not:

- turn the novel into generic dystopia
- flatten it into tech policy fiction
- make every conversation ideological
- solve emotional complexity with neat speeches
- treat spirituality as decorative garnish
- make the ending grandiose
- overuse jargon
- drift into action-movie pacing
- forget the trilogy's philosophical continuity
- replace human ambiguity with clean conceptual symmetry everywhere

This book should breathe.

## Preferred Creative Questions

When helping with this project, prioritize questions like:

- What emotional truth is this scene revealing?
- How does this connect to the trilogy's larger arc?
- What does this moment say about selfhood, empathy, or interbeing?
- Is this consequence believable at the human level?
- Where is the tension between consent and connection?
- How would this be interpreted differently across cultures?
- Does this feel earned?
- Is this too cynical or too neat?
- What would make this more intimate, more human, or more surprising?

## Guiding Thesis

A useful north star for this project is:

**The empathy machine did not create human interconnection. It only made it harder to ignore.**

And a useful emotional north star is:

**The final movement of the trilogy is not toward ecstasy, but toward awe, equanimity, and a quieter understanding that the distance between us was never as absolute as we believed.**

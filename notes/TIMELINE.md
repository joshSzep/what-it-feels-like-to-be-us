# Timeline

This timeline outlines the evolution of empath technology between the events of the first book and the beginning of *What It Feels Like to Be Us*.

The goal is not precise dates but a believable progression of technological and cultural change.

---

# Year 0 — The Breakthrough

The empathy engine is successfully demonstrated for the first time.

Researchers prove that emotional states can be shared between two participants through a technological interface.

The discovery attracts global attention.

Early reactions include:

- scientific excitement
- philosophical debate
- ethical concern

The technology remains restricted to research institutions.

---

# Years 1–3 — Early Research

Academic institutions begin studying the technology.

Controlled experiments explore:

- emotional resonance
- psychological safety
- therapeutic applications
- limits of emotional transmission

Governments begin drafting regulatory frameworks.

The public learns about the technology but has little direct access to it.

---

# Years 4–6 — Clinical Adoption

Empath technology begins appearing in limited professional settings.

Examples include:

- psychotherapy
- trauma counseling
- conflict mediation
- restorative justice programs

Early reports suggest the technology can significantly improve emotional understanding between participants.

At the same time, concerns arise about:

- emotional manipulation
- psychological overload
- consent and privacy

---

# Years 7–9 — Cultural Awareness

The concept of emotional sharing becomes widely known.

Media coverage expands.

Philosophers, ethicists, and religious leaders begin debating the implications.

Different countries adopt different regulatory models:

- strict clinical regulation
- total prohibition
- experimental adoption

At the edges of the system, underground engineers begin experimenting with unauthorized empath devices.

---

# Years 10–12 — Global Diffusion

Empath technology begins spreading beyond official channels.

Foreign labs and private developers replicate the technology.

Black markets begin distributing uncertified devices.

New cultural practices emerge:

- informal emoting between friends
- underground emoting gatherings
- experimentation with group emoting

The word **emoting** becomes common language.

---

# Years 13–14 — Early Network Experiments

Researchers begin exploring the possibility of connecting multiple participants simultaneously.

Small-scale emotional networks are tested with groups of:

- 5 participants
- 20 participants
- 50 participants

Unexpected phenomena appear.

Instead of emotional chaos, some networks begin stabilizing into shared emotional atmospheres.

Researchers cannot fully explain why.

---

# Year 15 — The Threshold

Engineers and researchers believe large-scale emotional networks may now be possible.

Several institutions propose coordinated global experiments.

Public debate intensifies.

Critics warn about mass emotional manipulation.

Supporters argue that large-scale empathy could transform human understanding.

A global emoting event begins to take shape.

---

# The Beginning of the Novel

The story begins as the world approaches the first truly global emoting experiment.

The technology has escaped the lab.

Regulation struggles to keep pace.

Society is divided.

And no one knows what will happen when millions of emotional signals connect at once.
